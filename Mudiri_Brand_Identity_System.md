# Mudiri (مديري) — Executive Brand Identity System

## 1. Brand Vision & Positioning
**Mudiri** is a premium, offline-first executive operating system designed specifically for CEOs, executive managers, and high-level offices. 

The brand identity must communicate:
*   **Authority & Trust:** A secure, impenetrable system for sensitive data.
*   **Executive Calm:** A noise-free, highly organized environment.
*   **Premium Minimalism:** Sophisticated simplicity over flashy trends.
*   **Arabic-First Excellence:** Rooted in modern Middle Eastern enterprise culture.

---

## 2. Core Visual Principles
Based on the approved **Mudiri Splash Screen** direction, all visual assets must adhere to these foundational rules:

*   **Executive Minimalism (Nano Banana Style):** Soft, premium, abstract, and calm. Avoid clutter, sharp harsh lines, or cartoonish elements.
*   **Soft Premium Geometry:** Use subtle, rounded geometric shapes inspired by modern interpretations of Arabic tessellation.
*   **Flat Vector Composition:** Absolutely **NO** heavy gradients, 3D renders, glassmorphism, or neon glow effects. Keep elements flat but layered using soft neumorphic shadows (already defined in the UI system).
*   **Generous Whitespace:** Give elements room to breathe. Density should be low to promote a feeling of calm control.

---

## 3. Color System
The color palette is restricted to maintain a professional, enterprise-grade feel.

### Primary Palette
*   **Primary Navy:** `#1E3A5F` — Used for primary logos, main text, and strong accents. Represents authority and trust.
*   **Executive Gold:** `#D4A373` — Used sparingly for subtle accents, loading indicators, and active states. Represents premium quality and leadership.

### Surface & Background (Light Mode)
*   **Background:** `#E8EDF2` — A soft, cool grey-blue.
*   **Surface:** `#EEF2F7` — Slightly lighter surface for cards and elevated elements.

### Surface & Background (Dark Mode)
*   **Background:** `#1E2530` — Deep, calm dark slate.
*   **Surface:** `#242D3A` — Elevated dark surface.

---

## 4. Typography System
*   **UI & Body Text:** `Tajawal` — Clean, modern, highly readable Arabic typeface.
*   **Headings & Wordmarks:** `Kufam` — Structured, authoritative, and balanced.

---

## 5. Unified Asset Guidelines
To unify all assets (Icons, Illustrations, and Monograms) with the Splash Screen design:

### 5.1. Logo & Monogram
*   **Structure:** A geometric, symmetrical monogram based on the Arabic letter "م" (Meem).
*   **Style:** Clean vector lines. The thickness of the lines should match the stroke weight of the typography used in the wordmark.
*   **Usage:** Centered compositions are preferred. The monogram should always be perfectly balanced.

### 5.2. App Icon & Adaptive Icon
*   **Background:** Solid Primary Navy (`#1E3A5F`).
*   **Foreground:** The central monogram in white (`#FFFFFF`) with a very subtle Executive Gold (`#D4A373`) accent.
*   **Rule:** The icon must remain flat. Do not add glossy reflections or drop shadows inside the icon mark itself.

### 5.3. Empty States & Onboarding Illustrations
*   **Style:** Abstract and geometric. 
*   **Composition:** Use soft shapes that subtly hint at office elements (desks, documents, meeting tables) without drawing them literally.
*   **Coloring:** Use the Background (`#E8EDF2`) as a canvas. Build shapes using Primary Navy (`#1E3A5F`) and Secondary Navy (`#274C77`), and use Executive Gold (`#D4A373`) as the focal point (e.g., a critical decision arrow or a highlighted document).

### 5.4. Iconography
*   **Family:** Consistent line-weight icons (e.g., Material Symbols or Lucide).
*   **Weight:** Regular to Light stroke weight to maintain elegance.
*   **Color:** Primary Navy (`#1E3A5F`) for inactive, Executive Gold (`#D4A373`) for active/selected.

---

## 6. The "Splash Screen" Standard
Whenever generating or designing a new asset for Mudiri, ask:
1.  *Is it flat and clean?*
2.  *Does it use only the Navy, Soft Grey, and Gold palette?*
3.  *Does it feel calm and executive, rather than playful or trendy?*

If the answer to any of these is no, the asset must be revised to match the approved Splash Screen aesthetic.
